talking about sex with your partner

But talking about sex is difficult! Why?

  • We're afraid that we might hurt our partner, or that they might be judged, scared or offended
  • We're afraid of being judged, "What will he think of me?"
  • We're embarrassed, "How could I possibly say THAT!?"
  • We're worried that our partner might misunderstand us or refuse a request
  • Often we don't even know what we want, but we know something could be better
  • Our upbringing makes us think we shouldn't be feeling/saying/wanting/doing this!

"Talking about sex can make us feel unprepared, scared and awkward which leads us to avoidence or defensiveness."

We lack the necessary skills for this conversation because we rarely practice similar high-emotionally charged discussions in our life. We even lack the vocabularly, because we usually don't talk in detail about sex openly with our friends.

More often than not, the real problem isn't that either of you doesn't want to please your partner, it's that we find it so difficult to tell each other. Talking about sex isn't a one off conversation. It needs to be nurtured over time, constantly part of your life so that you're both continue to learn.

Pillow Talk exists to change this!

About

This is just the beginning...

Pillow talk has been built on the premise that we do not talk about sex enough in society. We’re infatuated by it and it’s used to manipulate us, but we’re embarrassed to talk about it. By not talking about sex we give rise to a host of problems, including education and expectation.

Great sex doesn’t just happen, it needs to be carried out with skill and thoughtfulness. People’s tastes, preferences and values differ greatly and we hope Pillow Talk is a starting point to help bridge that communication gap between couples.

We believe technology can help us express ourselves in a way we have not imagined. We’re at the very beginning of our journey and we hope you get involved! How could we make this better? Tweet us! :)
